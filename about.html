Personal Profile




He is introduced as a chemist who graduated from the California Institute of Technology, who co-founded the company Gray Matter Technologies. He left Gray Matter abruptly, selling his shares for $5,000. Soon after, the company made a fortune, largely thanks to Walt's research. Walt later moved to Albuquerque, New Mexico, where he became a high school chemistry teacher. On his 50th birthday, he is diagnosed with stage IIIA lung cancer. After this discovery, Walt turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine with a former student of his, Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul), to ensure his family's financial security after his death. He becomes increasingly immersed in the illicit drug trade, becoming more ruthless as the series progresses, and later adopts the alias "Heisenberg", which becomes recognizable as a key figure in the Southwestern drug trade.

Work Experience













J. P. Wynne High School

1989 - 2007

Walter White, the central character in the television series "Breaking Bad," worked as a high school chemistry teacher at J. P. Wynne High School, a fictional educational institution set in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Despite his considerable expertise in chemistry, Walter faced financial struggles due to the relatively modest income associated with his teaching profession. To supplement his earnings, he took on a part-time job at the A1A Car Wash, showcasing the challenges of making ends meet. However, Walter's life took a dramatic turn when he received a terminal cancer diagnosis. Faced with the urgency of providing for his family's financial future, he entered the illicit world of methamphetamine production, utilizing his scientific knowledge to become a key player in the drug trade. The juxtaposition of Walter's initial role as an educator with his eventual descent into criminality forms the core narrative of "Breaking Bad."

A1A Car Wash

2001 - 2007

Walter White worked as a car wash at A1A Car Wash, a fictitious company located in Albuquerque, New Mexico, as part of his efforts to supplement his income as a chemistry teacher. This job at the car wash, which contrasted markedly with his experience and knowledge in chemistry, illustrates the economic challenges Walter faced. Although trained as a scientist, he was forced to take on less specialized work to make ends meet and cope with increasing financial pressures that eventually led him to make drastic decisions in the "Breaking Bad" storyline.

Other business

2007 - September 7, 2010

Walter White began working with Gustavo Fring in the drug trafficking business in "Breaking Bad." Under the façade of an industrial laundry company called "Los Pollos Hermanos", Gus Fring hid his extensive methamphetamine empire. Walter, along with his partner Jesse Pinkman, became involved in drug production in a laboratory hidden under the laundry room. This agreement was established around the second season of the series, after Walter and Jesse began to face challenges and threats in the drug market. The working relationship with Gus Fring provided Walter with an efficient infrastructure for the manufacturing and distribution of methamphetamines, although tensions and conflicts developed between the two characters throughout the series.
